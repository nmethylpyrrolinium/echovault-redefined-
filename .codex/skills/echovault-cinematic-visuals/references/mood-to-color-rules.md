# Mood-to-Color Rules

Use mood color to support emotional meaning while keeping EchoVault cohesive.

| Mood | Core color | Accent | Motion density |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| calm | mist blue `#9DB7FF` | lavender `#C8B6FF` | low |
| joy | sunrise gold `#FFD37A` | rose `#FF8FB3` | medium |
| sadness | deep blue `#5472D3` | silver `#BFD7FF` | low |
| anger | ember `#FF6A4A` | violet shadow `#8B5CF6` | medium-low |
| anxiety | teal `#73E6D4` | cool blue `#8AB4FF` | low, no jitter |
| hope | aurora green `#8EF0B1` | gold `#FFE08A` | medium |
| love | rose `#FF8DBA` | warm violet `#C084FC` | medium-low |
| nostalgia | amber `#D8A85A` | dusk blue `#8796C8` | low |

## Usage

- Build gradients from core color at low opacity plus neutral dark bases.
- Keep text contrast independent from glow color.
- Use accent color for highlights, constellation endpoints, or reveal flashes only.
