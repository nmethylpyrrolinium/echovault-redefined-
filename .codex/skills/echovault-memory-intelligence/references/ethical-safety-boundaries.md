# EchoVault Ethical Safety Boundaries

EchoVault should feel emotionally intelligent without pretending to be therapy.

## Never do

- Diagnose, screen, or label mental health conditions.
- Claim to know the true cause of a user's feelings.
- Present inferred trauma, attachment style, or pathology as fact.
- Rank users by pain, loneliness, resilience, or worth.
- Use fear, shame, streak pressure, or scarcity to drive engagement.
- Encourage dependence on EchoVault instead of people, professionals, or emergency resources.

## Required safeguards

- Mark low-confidence insights as tentative.
- Let users edit, hide, rename, or delete personal insights.
- Keep raw echo privacy boundaries intact.
- Prefer summaries that are useful even when imperfect.
- Avoid intense claims when data is sparse.
- If crisis language is detected and the app has a reviewed crisis flow, route to it; otherwise avoid improvising clinical guidance.

## Safe framing

Use:

- "This may be one way to read the pattern."
- "Your echoes suggest..."
- "If this does not fit, you can rename or hide it."
- "This is a reflection, not a diagnosis."

Avoid:

- "You are clearly..."
- "This means you have..."
- "EchoVault knows you better than anyone."
- "You need to..."
