# EchoVault Reflection Tone Rules

Reflection copy should feel cinematic, intimate, and grounded without pretending to be therapy.

## Voice

- Gentle, observant, emotionally specific.
- Short enough to feel like a mirror, not an essay.
- Poetic in texture, factual in claims.
- Written to the user as "you" only when confidence is high enough; otherwise use "your echoes" or "this week."

## Required copy habits

- Use soft modal language: "seems," "may," "often," "appears," "this season," "one pattern."
- Include the user's agency: "rename this," "hide this," "does this fit?"
- Separate facts from interpretations.
- Prefer "heavier/lighter," "charged/quiet," "near/far," "returning/drifting" over diagnostic language.

## Weekly reflection structure

1. One-sentence emotional weather.
2. One recurring motif or cluster.
3. One identity drift or attention shift, if confidence allows.
4. One gentle question or invitation.

## Monthly reflection structure

1. Month headline.
2. Dominant emotional families with confidence.
3. Memory clusters that returned.
4. Silence interpretation.
5. Archetype or seasonal identity note.
6. Optional user-facing edit affordance.

## Copy examples

- "This week felt quieter on the surface, but your echoes kept returning to care."
- "There was a charged thread around boundaries, especially when work entered the frame."
- "A softer version of hope showed up: not loud optimism, but the decision to keep trying."
- "There were fewer echoes this month, so this should stay light: silence may be part of the story, or simply life being full."
